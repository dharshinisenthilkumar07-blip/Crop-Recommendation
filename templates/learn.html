About Agriculture

Agriculture is the practice of cultivating plants and livestock. It is the backbone of food supply and plays a major role in economic development and sustainability.

Agriculture in India

India is one of the world's largest producers of rice, wheat, cotton, and pulses. Agriculture employs over 50% of India's population and contributes significantly to its GDP.

Soil Health

Soil health is essential for crop yield. Key indicators include pH, organic matter, moisture, and NPK levels. Good soil management ensures long-term productivity.

Sustainable Farming

Sustainable agriculture includes crop rotation, minimal pesticide use, water conservation, and organic practices to preserve biodiversity and reduce carbon impact.

Agri Policies & Constitution

Article 48 of the Indian Constitution promotes modern agricultural practices. Policies like PM-Kisan, eNAM, and the Soil Health Card Scheme support farmers across the country.

Technology in Agriculture

Technologies such as AI, sensors, drones, and precision farming are transforming agriculture by improving efficiency, reducing waste, and increasing crop quality.

Major Crops & Their Requirements

Wheat grows best in cool, dry climates and loamy soil. Rice needs plenty of water and clayey soil. Sugarcane thrives in tropical regions with rich alluvial soil and requires high water input.

Fertilizer Types & Usage

Fertilizers supply essential nutrients like nitrogen (urea), phosphorus (DAP), and potassium (MOP). Organic options like compost and green manure improve soil structure and biodiversity.
